Problem Solving skills: 

If you have the misconception that merely knowing a programming language is what it takes to be a software developer then you are wrong. Software developers will be faced with complex challenges and they have to come up with innovative solutions on a daily basis. 

In context to software developing, problem-solving is an extremely important skill. A software developer will be dealing with multiple issues with respect to what they are working on. This could be identifying a bug, understanding errors in functionality or a specific requirement from clients. They will have to use their theoretical knowledge, logical thinking and creativity to come up with solutions for the problems. To generate effective solutions for problems, Developers have to break down the problem into smaller and more manageable components to create solutions for them. They will have to collaborate with stakeholders, other developers, designers to select the best course of action for the problem they are trying to solve. 

This is a quality that can be developed over time. If you're an individual who loves to solve problems then this industry can be most suitable for you.

Creative Thinking skills: 

Software developers will be required to come with creative ideas to solve problems. Every software is built to solve a particular problem faced by users and creating such a solution takes a lot of creativity. Writing code in itself is a creative process. If a software developer is writing code exactly like that in the previous problem then they just need to copy and paste it, but that’s not how it works. Every single problem needs a different approach to coding.

Only the best creative minds offer good solutions for problems. This is also a quality that you can develop. The first and foremost step is to be curious and be open to ideas. Most often creativity is a result of experimenting a lot of unique solutions. If you are ready to experiment with unique ideas for the problem you are dealing with then creativity will automatically come to you.

Logical Thinking 

The whole process of writing code demands strong logical thinking skills. A programmer must know how to use logic to write code that fills the actual requirement. This is a quality that comes as a subset to problem-solving. To solve any problem, individuals will have to break down the big problem into small chunks of manageable tasks.

From analyzing the problem to formulating potential solutions to them, it needs a strong logical thinking to approach everything. Developing a strong logical thinking skill is crucial for any aspiring developer.

Continuous learning 

This is an ever-changing field. If a person is not up-to-date with the latest skills and technologies then they will soon become irrelevant. A learning mindset is a quality to develop if you want to build a long term career on software development.

These are some pre-requisites that you need to consider before stepping into this industry. A great future awaits for anyone who is ready to master this skill. 

Software development is not an easy skill to learn. You really have to be willing to invest a significant amount of time and hard work to master this skill. The fact that this is a hard to learn skill is what makes it a high paying career. Initially to learn the skill, it takes quite a lot of time but once you get a hold over it then it becomes fun. There is a huge potential to earn money in this field. Good software developers are always on demand and companies are willing to pay hefty amounts for them.

Do you need a degree to work in MNCs?

MNCs are known for mass hiring, they go to colleges and recruit graduates fresh out of college. The fact that you need to understand here is that they don’t recruit based on skills. MNCs clearly know that these graduates don't have any skills to perform in a company. They take these students to their campuses, and then they will be trained on relevant skills. The main reason why they do this is that they are not able to find such numerous skilled employees. So, the only option they are left with is to train candidates and then give them job responsibilities.  

This is not the case for startups and other small service based companies. MNCs have all the resources to train students, but this is not a luxury startup and other small companies can afford. They will be in search of candidates who are skilled and can start working without prior training. 

Degree is no longer a barrier to start your career as a software developer. If you have the technical skill, and you know how to showcase that to your potential employer then getting a job is not difficult.

Step-by-step guide to become a software developer:

Software development is not just a profession where you can master technical skills and do the job. Just like any other profession, soft skills are crucial to work properly in an organization. Here are 4 soft skill to master as a software developer.

Step 1: Understand the basics of coding

A proper understanding of coding will set a solid foundation for your software developing career. This is the most crucial and important step on your journey as a software developer. Start by learning fundamental concepts like variables, conditional statements and loops. Once you are clear with the fundamental concepts you can choose a beginner-friendly programming language of your choice and start writing simple programs. Practice a lot and slowly move on to

more complicated programs.  Once you have a proper grasp over the basics of coding. You're now in a position to make further decisions regarding what particular field you want to work on. You don’t need to do any courses to learn the basics. Ample of resources are available in online platforms like YouTube, Udemy, edx. 

If you need a comprehensive video series to understand the basics of coding, you can try out the 100K Coding Challenge(hyperlink). Watch the videos and experiment with everything mentioned in the video, your basics will be clear.

Step 2: Plan your career

After spending some time learning the basics, you will now know if coding is your cup of tea or not. If you are clear that software development is what you should do then now it’s time to plan your career.

Figure out how you are going to achieve the goal of becoming a software developer. You can choose to join a course or do self learning.

Gain a proper understanding about different domains in software development and choose the one that is right for you. Once you pick a domain, stick to it till you gain good experience on it. Many beginners make the mistake of trying to learn different domains.

Give a proper timeline for yourself to master this skill and plan everything out. Figure out a basic plan on how you are going to learn everything.

Step 3: Build Projects

You can start building projects after learning the skills. Working on projects will further improve your overall programming skills. Having skills is not enough, you have to show the recruiters that you are worthy of becoming a software developer in that company. Projects you do will showcase your potential and skills.

Step 4: Start applying

Once you are done with all these three steps then the next step is to start applying. Build an attractive resume including all your skills and projects. Initially when you start applying, you will face a lot of rejection. Many might get demotivated during this time you have to stay consistent. All it takes is one job to start your software developing career.
